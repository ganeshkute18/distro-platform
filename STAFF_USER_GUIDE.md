## Getting Started

### First Login

1. **You'll receive email** from your owner with:
   - Email address for login
   - Temporary password
   - Link to DistroPro app
2. **Go to DistroPro login page**
3. **Sign in** with your email and password
4. **Change your password** on first login (create your own secure password)
5. **Allow notifications** when prompted
6. **Welcome!** You're now on Staff Dashboard

### What's Your Job?

As **Staff**, your responsibilities are:

- ✅ **Process Approved Orders**: Prepare packages for delivery
- ✅ **Update Order Status**: Track order from processing → dispatched → delivered
- ✅ **Manage Inventory**: Update stock levels, manage low-stock items
- ✅ **Handle Payments**: Process payment receipts from customers
- ✅ **Maintain Records**: Keep accurate notes on orders and stock

## Order Processing

### Order Workflow You Handle

```
APPROVED Order (from Owner)
    ↓ (YOUR WORK STARTS HERE)
📦 PROCESSING (You're preparing it)
    ↓
📮 DISPATCHED (You've sent it out)
    ↓
✅ DELIVERED (Customer received it)
```

### Processing an Approved Order

**What You Do When Owner Approves**:

1. **Go to Dashboard** → **"Orders to Process"** tab
2. **Click order** to see details:
   - Customer name & address
   - Items ordered (quantities)
   - Special instructions (if any)
3. **Prepare the order**:
   - Gather items from warehouse
   - Verify quantities match order
   - Check for any defects
   - Pack safely
4. **Mark as PROCESSING**:
   - Click **"Mark as Processing"** button
   - Add notes if any: (e.g., "Packed at 10:30 AM", "Item replaced - out of stock")
   - Save

### Updating to DISPATCHED

**When order is ready to ship**:

1. **Go to Orders** → Find the order
2. **Click order** → Current status shown
3. **Click "Update Status" → "Dispatched"**
4. **Add dispatch details**:
   - Dispatch time
   - Courier/carrier name (if using)
   - Tracking number (if available)
   - Any special notes
5. **Click "Save"**
6. **Customer receives notification** automatically

### Marking as DELIVERED

**When customer confirms receipt**:

1. **Go to Orders** → Find the order
2. **Click order** → See current status
3. **Click "Update Status" → "Delivered"**
4. **Confirm details**:
   - Delivery date
   - Customer signature/confirmation
   - Any issues? Add notes
5. **Click "Mark Delivered"**
6. **Order closes** - Revenue recorded

### Bulk Order Processing

If you have many orders:

1. Go to **Orders** → **"Processing"** tab
2. Use filters:
   - By date
   - By customer
   - By status
3. Process several orders quickly
4. Check off as you go

## Inventory Management

### Your Stock Responsibility

As staff, you're the eyes and ears of inventory:

- 👀 **Know what's in warehouse**
- 🔢 **Update stock levels accurately**
- ⚠️ **Flag low stock items**
- 🎯 **Plan reorders**

### Viewing Current Inventory

**To see what's available**:

1. **Go to Inventory** section
2. **See all products** with:
   - Product name
   - Current stock level
   - Reserved stock (in pending orders)
   - Available stock (ready to sell)
   - Threshold level

### Updating Stock Levels

**When you receive new goods**:

1. **Go to Inventory**
2. **Find the product**
3. **Click "Update Stock"**
4. **Enter new quantity**:
   - Example: "Previous: 50 units" → "New: 150 units"
   - System calculates addition automatically
5. **Add notes** (optional):
   - e.g., "Received shipment from supplier ABC"
   - e.g., "Physical count done, adjusted from 40 to 38"
6. **Click "Save Update"**
7. **Change is recorded** (Owner can see when stock was updated)

### Understanding Stock Status

```
Example: Fresh Milk
─────────────────────────────────
Total Stock:        100 units
Reserved:           - 25 units (in pending orders)
Available:          = 75 units (can be sold)
Low Threshold:      30 units
Status:             🟢 Healthy (75 > 30)

If sold 50 units:
─────────────────────────────────
Available:          25 units
Status:             🔴 LOW STOCK ALERT! (25 < 30)
Your Action:        Flag for reorder
```

### Low Stock Alerts

**How to Handle Low Stock**:

1. **See Alert** on dashboard or inventory
2. **Review product details**:
   - Current stock
   - Threshold
   - Recent sales rate
3. **Notify Owner**:
   - Use notification system
   - Or tell verbally if in same location
4. **Owner will** arrange reorder with supplier

### Physical Stock Verification

**Weekly Task**:

1. **Go to physical warehouse**
2. **Count actual items** for 5-10 products
3. **Compare with system**
4. **Discrepancy?**
   - If system shows 50 but actual is 45 → Update to 45
   - Add note: "Physical count, found discrepancy"
5. **Update system** with corrected numbers

## Payment Handling

### Processing Payment Receipts

**When customer pays**:

1. **Customer uploads receipt** (screenshot/photo of payment)
2. **You receive notification** → "Payment receipt pending review"
3. **Go to Orders** → Click the order
4. **See payment details**:
   - Receipt image
   - Payment method used
   - Amount paid
   - Timestamp
5. **Verify payment**:
   - Check amount matches order total
   - Confirm payment method
   - Look for any issues
6. **Actions**:
   - ✅ **Verify**: Payment confirmed, order continues
   - ❌ **Reject**: Payment looks suspicious, ask customer for clarification

### Recording Manual Payments

**For cash or direct bank transfers**:

1. **Go to Orders**
2. **Find the order**
3. **Click "Add Payment"**
4. **Select payment method**:
   - Cash
   - Bank Transfer
   - Check
5. **Enter amount**
6. **Add reference** (e.g., check number, transaction ID)
7. **Save**
8. **Owner can review** in reporting

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Order Missing an Item

**What Happened**: You're processing an order but can't find one item. It's in the list but not in warehouse.

**Your Steps**:

1. **Don't panic** - this happens
2. **Check locations**:
   - Misplaced in warehouse?
   - Moved to another section?
3. **Still can't find?**
   - **Check inventory**: System shows 0 or low?
   - If 0: Item is out of stock (already sold/committed)
4. **Add note to order**:
   - "Item XYZ not found in warehouse. Possible out of stock."
   - "Contact owner for decision"
5. **Don't ship incomplete order** without owner approval
6. **Owner will decide**:
   - Substitute with similar item?
   - Partial shipment + remainder later?
   - Refund that item?

### Scenario 2: Customer Returns Item

**What Happened**: Customer says item arrived damaged/wrong.

**Your Steps**:

1. **Get details**: What's wrong? Can it be fixed?
2. **Inspect the item**:
   - Damage visible? Take photo
   - Wrong product? Compare order
3. **Document everything**:
   - Go to order
   - Add notes: "Customer returned item, reason: [...]"
   - Attach photo if possible
4. **Notify owner immediately**:
   - Use messages/calls
   - Owner decides on replacement/refund
5. **Process decision**:
   - If replacement: Ship new item
   - If refund: Owner processes credit
6. **Update order** with resolution

### Scenario 3: Low Stock While Processing Order

**What Happened**: Order approved for 50 units but warehouse only has 30.

**Your Steps**:

1. **Find the order** (in Processing queue)
2. **Check actual stock**:
   - Is warehouse count wrong? (Physical count)
   - Or inventory already updated?
3. **Add note to order**:
   - "Only 30 units available, customer ordered 50"
4. **Notify owner immediately**:
   - Don't hold up the order
   - Owner decides:
     - Partial shipment (30 units)?
     - Wait for restock?
     - Cancel that part?
5. **Follow owner's decision**
6. **Update inventory** accordingly

### Scenario 4: Payment Discrepancy

**What Happened**: Customer uploaded receipt showing ₹500 but order total is ₹600.

**Your Steps**:

1. **Check the receipt image** carefully
   - Full amount visible?
   - Multiple transactions? (Maybe they already paid partial?)
2. **Check order history**:
   - Was there a previous partial payment?
   - See payment timeline
3. **Calculate**: How much is still due?
4. **Actions**:
   - If clear mistake: Contact customer to send full amount
   - If partial payment accepted: Mark as partial, owner handles
   - If dispute: Don't proceed, wait for owner decision
5. **Add detailed notes**:
   - "Receipt shows ₹500, order is ₹600. Customer paid 83%. Awaiting full payment."
6. **Notify owner** if amount is significant

## Inventory Best Practices

### Daily Habit

✅ **Every time you touch an item**:
- Update inventory immediately
- Don't wait until end of day
- Real-time = accurate data

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Wrong quantities**:
- Example: Update says "50 units" but you meant "Added 50 units"
- Always be clear if adding, replacing, or correcting

❌ **Forgotten updates**:
- Items damaged/spoiled but never removed from system
- Customers can't order items that don't actually exist

❌ **No notes**:
- Owner doesn't know why stock changed
- Hard to audit if something seems off

❌ **Conflicting updates**:
- Two people updating same product simultaneously
- Refresh page before updating to avoid conflicts

### Best Practices

✅ **Add detailed notes**: "Received shipment from Supplier ABC, 100 units"

✅ **Update immediately**: Don't batch updates, do it when it happens

✅ **Verify before updating**: Confirm quantity before hitting save

✅ **Use consistent language**: Owner can understand what you mean

✅ **Physical counts**: Verify system accuracy regularly

## Productivity Tips

### Work Faster

⚡ **Use Filters**:
- Orders by date/customer
- Inventory by category
- Payments by status

⚡ **Keyboard Shortcuts** (if available):
- Tab to move between fields
- Enter to confirm
- ESC to cancel

⚡ **Batch Similar Tasks**:
- Process 10 orders at once (not switching back/forth)
- Update all low-stock alerts together
- Verify all pending payments at once

### Quality Over Speed

📋 **Accuracy matters more** than being fast:
- One wrong order is worse than 10 correct ones
- Double-check important numbers
- Better to be 10% slower and 100% accurate
